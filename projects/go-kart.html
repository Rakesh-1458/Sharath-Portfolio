Go-Kart Design

Role Chassis Designer & FEA Analyst
Timeline February 2026
Core Stack SolidWorks / ANSYS / Drivetrain Tuning
Category Vehicle Dynamics

Project Overview

I designed and structurally optimized a high-performance electric Go-Kart chassis. My primary focus was on driver safety, mass minimization, and cornering performance. Through structural optimization and drivetrain calibration, I aimed to maximize acceleration while ensuring high torsional rigidity to withstand heavy cornering loads.

Chassis Engineering & FEA Validation

I drafted the spaceframe chassis in SolidWorks, selecting high-strength AISI 4130 chromoly steel tubing for its optimal strength-to-weight ratio. To validate safety and verify structural compliance, I imported the CAD frame into ANSYS and conducted rigorous Finite Element Analysis (FEA) simulating frontal impact, rear collision, side impact, and torsional warp.

By analyzing stress distribution, I identified areas with high concentration (shear points at gussets and motor mounts). I then selectively altered tube diameters and Wall-thickness, and introduced triangular support links. This iterative process allowed me to shave off 9 kg from the chassis (a 40% weight reduction) while preserving structural integrity and keeping the factor of safety above 2.0.

Drivetrain Tuning & Performance

On the drivetrain side, I calibrated the sprocket and gear ratios to match the torque characteristics of our 48V brushless DC motor. I performed motor sizing and weight-distribution calculations to optimize the traction limit of the tires.

Through gear-ratio modifications, I tuned the drivetrain to achieve rapid acceleration, launching the kart from 0 to 100 meters in just 5.4 seconds during validation runs, balancing peak speed against acceleration punch.

Key Deliverables & Achievements

  • Designed and modeled a complete Go-Kart chassis spaceframe in SolidWorks, achieving an optimized weight savings of 9 kg.
  • Conducted full-vehicle FEA tests (ANSYS) for frontal impact, rear crash, and torsional load cases.
  • Calibrated motor-gear ratios to achieve a tested 0-100m acceleration sprint of 5.4 seconds.

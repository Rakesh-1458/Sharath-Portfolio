Internship Overview

During my 5-month internship at Kinetron Technologies, I worked hands-on to prototype, refine, and validate advanced tactical unmanned aerial vehicles (UAVs). Moving from mechanical blueprints to hardware integration and flight testing, I focused on high-reliability systems designed to withstand environmental challenges and execute complex aerial maneuvers.

1. VTOL Tilt Motor Mechanism

One of my primary responsibilities was engineering a custom tilt-motor mechanism for Vertical Take-Off and Landing (VTOL) drone platforms. The core challenge was achieving a smooth, rapid 90-degree transition from vertical hover to horizontal cruising flight while maintaining absolute structural rigidity under full motor thrust.

I designed carbon fiber pushrods and aluminum-alloy linkages in SolidWorks, validating their strengths through FEA simulation. Driven by high-torque, metal-gear servos, the mechanism successfully minimized play at the hinge joint and damped airframe vibrations by 75% using alpha-gel vibration isolators.

2. Waterproof Drone Platform

To enable reliable operations in adverse weather conditions, I contributed to the mechanical enclosure styling of an IP67-rated waterproof drone shell. I designed custom EPDM O-ring gaskets along joint interfaces, ensuring a watertight seal for all internal compartments housing sensitive electronics.

To balance internal barometric pressure (essential for altitude-hold sensors) with waterproofing, I integrated micro-porous Gore-Tex venting membranes. Additionally, internal ESC heat dissipation was managed by linking their thermal blocks to the exterior carbon fiber frame via conductive aluminum heatsinks.

3. Spying Helicopter Development

I assisted in integrating mechanical subsystems, payloads, and telemetry for tactical surveillance helicopter models. This involved the layout of a Hiller flybarless cyclic pitch rotorhead, calibrating dual-redundant GPS receivers, and setting up real-time telemetry links.

I designed a lightweight 2-axis brushless camera stabilization gimbal capable of carrying a 500g tactical optical zoom sensor, linking its controller to the ArduPilot flight computer. The final integration was validated through structured autonomous test flights to inspect waypoint accuracy and video telemetry latency over long ranges.
